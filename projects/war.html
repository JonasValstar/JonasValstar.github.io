In this project, I wanted to learn how to do procedural animation as well as learn how GameMaker works. I have always liked the idea of procedural animation so wanted to put that in the game, which my team thought would also be a good idea.


I also made a system for ordering NPC characters around, to solve the puzzles with their unique abilities. One of these puzzles that I also made being a laser redirection puzzle.


Overall, the project was great for learning GameMaker and its strengths, quirks and limitations.

For the procedurally animated spider legs I made a system that would keep track of 2 points. An origin point moving with the character and a feet point staying on the ground.


The system would check every frame if the angle between the origin and feet point (relative to the origins forward direction) is too big, or the distance between them is too big or small. If that is the case, it would move the feet point to a new location based on the movement direction of the player.


If, for some reason, the new location of the feet point is invalid it would pick the closest furthest possible location in the direction it initially wanted to go. For that I used a ray cast. But since GameMaker didn’t have that build in at the time, I made it myself based on binary search.

The player could also select the npc’s and give them orders to solve puzzles. I made a system where the player could click on portraits on the side of the screen to select the NPC they want to control, something like Baldur’s Gate 3 also has.


If the player clicked somewhere on the ground, the NPC would then walk towards that position. If the player clicked on something, it would walk there and interact with it if they could.

Version 1

One of the puzzles was a classic laser redirection puzzle. A laser emitter would shoot out a laser and mirrors could reflect it 90 degrees left or right to get to an end point.


If the emitter was activated, it would check for every tile in front of it if there was a mirror. If there was, it would check if it would bounce left or right. This repeats until the laser collides with a wall or end point.
